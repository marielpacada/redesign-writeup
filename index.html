ukutabs redesign

a redesign of a website that offers free ukulele chords

Introduction

Ukutabs.com is a website that hosts ukulele chord sheets for a wide range of songs. The main components of its homepage include a navigation bar, a masthead, and its content body. While it has the essential ingredients for a good home page, its layout could compromise the site's usability.

Here are some problems I observed -- take a look!

Low-Fidelity Prototyping

My first step in redesigning the site was to make low-fidelity prototypes. As mentioned above, most of the website's problems lie in its layout; thus, my solutions focus on improving Ukutabs's learnability for first time users. At this stage of my process, I designed my tablet layout to be identical to my desktop one. Expectedly, some of the elements would occupy less padding or width because of the smaller screen, but the essence of the layout remains the same. On the other hand, the mobile layout deviates a bit more. Unlike desktop and tablet, mobile does not have the anchor links in the navbar; instead, the navbar will host the logo and a button that will show the dropdown menu items upon click, pushing the current content down to accommodate for the menu.

The new navbar with anchor links as its items gives the site its own identity -- the user will more easily gather that ukutabs is its own webpage. The new locations for the dropdown menu and the search bar will improve user learnability and memorability, as these commonly-used elements are now in more prominent spaces on the page. Most importantly, the overall layout redesign will allow the user to discriminate between the types of information on the webpage, improving user efficiency.

High-Fidelity Prototyping

Transitioning from lofi to hifi prototyping, I continued to hone in on an optimal user experience. Realizing that my original plan to push the content down to accommodate for the dropdown menu could be disorienting to the user, I decided to implement a collapsing side menu instead. Also, I realized that most tablet sizes could not accommodate for the original desktop navbar; thus, I designed my tablet render to have the collapsing side menu as well.

The desktop layout remained relatively unchanged.
  • The navigation bar is a bootstrap navbar with a logo and navitem elements.
  • The masthead is a column-oriented flexbox.
  • On focus, the search bar's border switches to blue.
  • On hover,
    • the logo changes to a darker yellow font color.
    • the navitems change from white to gray.
    • the triangle next to the logo shows a menu.
  • The hover dropdown menu is a simple block display.
  • On hover, the background color of each menu item will change to a darker shade of green.
  • Each body section is a column-oriented flexbox with inner row-oriented flexboxes that wrap when needed.
  • The outer flexbox contains the title and the inner flexbox.
  • The inner flexbox contains the information for each section.
  • The songs are arranged in tables.
  • On hover,
    • The titles of each table will turn a darker shade of gray.
    • The artist name will turn a darker shade of red.
    • The song title will turn a lighter shade of red.
  • On hover,
    • the book icons increase in brightness to emulate illumination.
    • the arrow icon turns to white.
  • The song table here is identical to ones in the first body section.
  • The embedded YouTube video and the genres are also arranged in tables, featuring a header and only one row.
  • On hover, the genre links turn gray.
  • On hover, the icons (for both UkuWorld and social media) decrease in brightness.
There were some considerable changes to the tablet design.
  • Deviating from the desktop design, the tablet render will not feature a navigation with anchor links.
  • Instead, the tablet render features a collapsing side menu.
  • In the top left corner, there exists a button that opens a side menu.
    • This essentially replaces the hover dropdown in the desktop design.
  • On hover,
    • the button background changes to a darker shade of yellow.
    • the triangle in the button changes to white.
  • On click,
    • the button switches to grayscale to signify that it cannot be reclicked.
    • the side menu opens, appearing on top of the current design.
  • The side menu has fixed position and can be closed only by clicking the (x) button in its top right corner.
    • After closing the side menu, the button returns to its original colors.
  • The designs for the body sections remain fundamentally unchanged. Because the page will be organized in flexboxes that wrap, the content will automatically shift to fit within the given screen size.
There mobile design now closely resembles the tablet design.
  • Not much difference between tablet and mobile. The logo now takes up the width of the screen -- that's about it!

Final Design

This brings us to the final design! Coming out of the prototyping process, I made some last-minute tweaks, related mostly to things like centering text or increasing color contrast. I also added a scroll-to-top gif to provide the user with an easy way to scroll back up to search a song or to access the dropdown menu. Make sure to check out the final design! Takeaways

I had so much fun completing this project! Through this redesign, I learned the importance of planning out what you are going to build before you build it. The prototyping process allowed me not only to step into the users' shoes but also to walk in them. I have to admit -- the fact that I am a ukulele player who uses this website quite often certainly helped me in this process. However, the skills I developed and strategies I learned are most definitely applicable to future projects!
